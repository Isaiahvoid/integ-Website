Slow Progress Is Still Progress

BY: ISAAC BASCOS - JAN 28, 2026
It's not about how fast you go, but how consistent you are.

Debugging My Expectations

What if I told you that sometimes, no matter how much I study, it still feels like I’m falling behind? No matter how much I try, I struggle to understand the lessons because of my slow learning pace, while everyone else seems ahead of me. Even though it’s already my second year, there are days when I feel left behind while my classmates grasp concepts faster. It makes me wonder if I’ll really be ready for a career in CS someday. But even with all these doubts, I still believe I can improve, learn programming better, and slowly find my place in this field.

Learning at My Own Clock Speed

The Skills I’m Still Working On

   One of the hardest parts of CS for me is programming, especially when it comes to logical thinking and problem solving. There are still many times when I don’t understand a problem right away. While some of my classmates can write code quickly and solve tasks almost instantly, I often need more time just to figure out the logic. Sometimes it even takes me hours to fully understand a single problem. Because of that, I have moments where I feel frustrated and pressured, and I start questioning myself, wondering if I’m just a slow learner who can’t keep up.

   As time goes by, I’m realizing that struggling and making mistakes are normal parts of learning. Failure isn’t a reason for me to give up, it’s a reminder to keep pushing myself toward my goals and future. Instead of avoiding difficult problems like I used to, I’m trying to face them step by step, even if I don’t get the right answer on my first try. I’m learning that progress may be slow, but it’s still progress, and that’s what really matters. And as I think about what comes next, I can’t help but wonder how these lessons will shape the career I want to build and the skills I still need to master.

Compiling a Future Worth Having

   Looking back, I realize that my journey in CS is not just about becoming good at programming, but about learning how to be patient with myself. Struggling with lessons, taking longer to understand problems, and making mistakes used to make me feel discouraged, but now I see them as part of my growth. My biggest takeaway is that progress doesn’t have to be fast to be meaningful. I’m slowly learning to stop comparing myself to others and focus on improving little by little, at my own pace.

For the people who made this journey possible.

   As for the future, I still don’t know exactly where this course will lead me. If I end up working in the tech industry, I’ll be proud because I know how hard I worked to get there. But even if my path turns out differently, my goal is simple: to have a stable job, be financially stable, and build a life I can be proud of. One of my biggest dreams is to repay my parents for all the support, sacrifices, and encouragement they gave me throughout my journey as a CS student. They are the reason I’m able to study at a university, and I want to give back to them someday, not just financially, but by showing them that all their hard work was worth it. No matter where I end up, I want to carry the resilience, patience, and determination I’m learning now, because those are the things that will truly help me move forward.
